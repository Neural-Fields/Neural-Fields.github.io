Neural Fields in Principle

Workshop 2023


Workshop in conjunction with NeurIPS 2023
Sunday June 18th, 2023       New Orleans, USA

Overview

As an expansion of physical fields into the realm of artificial intelligence, neural fields have shown intriguing success in computer vision, computer graphic, robotics, etc. However, while existing works have focused on demonstrating the capabilities of neural fields, there has been less emphasis on understanding the fundamental principles that underlie the observed experimental phenomena. By drawing inspiration from physical fields where numerous fundamental principles has been rigorously derived and built, this workshop aims to foster discussions and uncover the essential principles and properties of neural fields that drive the observed experimental phenomena. This workshop invites researchers to delve into the foundational aspects and underlying mechanisms behind the success of neural fields, which could help to lay a sound foundation for the employment of neural fields in broad areas.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions of both short and long papers (4 pages and 8 pages respectively excluding references).
The long papers will be included in the proceedings of NeurIPS.
Potential topics include but are not limited to:

  • Advances in neural fields, including NeRF and neural rendering.
  • Encoding function and coordinate transformation in neural fields.
  • MLP-based neural fields versus Grid-based neural fields.
  • Smoothness and robustness of neural fields.
  • Generative models in neural fields.
  • Analysis-by-synthesis with neural fields.
  • Label transfer and correspondence learning in neural fields.
  • Specific properties in 2D, 3D, 4D, etc., neural fields.
  • Neural fields for science.
  • Ethical considerations in neural fields.
